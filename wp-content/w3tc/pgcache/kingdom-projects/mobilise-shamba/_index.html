Mobilise Shamba

Project Shamba is aimed to improve the conditions of the local primary school.  Shamba Primary School is in Zimbabwe, Midlands Province in Shurugwi District.  It is in Mwedzi Village under headman Mazivisa and  was established in 1911. Initially there were 2 teachers but this has grown to 23 teachers and over 650 pupils.

The school does not enjoy any support from government or any NGO, but is doing well despite economic challenges and had an average pass rate of 73%.  It has a garden and it uses a community borehole which is about 400m from the school. The school needs assistance to assist vulnerable children whose parents/guardians cannot meet their educational needs.  A good number of pupils are orphans because of HIV/AIDS.

The aims of  Project Shamba are:

  • To support the development of Shamba School by improving its infrastructure and teaching aids.
  • To support the training and development of teachers and  volunteers.
  • To support curriculum development and development of extra curriculum development activities i.e. sports, drama, music and other school activities.
  • To support youth development and adult literacy education to improve life-skills especially among women.
  • To support the school income generation effort for the purpose of increasing the sustainability of the school.
  • To raise awareness of HIV/AIDS through education and by increasing HIV testing and counselling to reduce stigma of HIV, and encourage people to know their status to increase access to treatment.
  • To support orphans and vulnerable children and young adults to acquire education and health.
  • To ensure that all our work is environmentally friendly in keeping with the global efforts on climate change and environmental sustainability.

Here are the objectives:

Objective 1: To refurbish the existing buildings to acceptable standards, these are 7 classrooms, 8 teacher’s accommodation and the school office.

The refurbishment should be done to Ministry of Construction acceptable standards. The refurbishment of these buildings involves the repairs to classrooms, school office and teacher’s accommodation. These buildings have lost window panes, doors, some have the roof blown away by the wind, the floors need repay and there is need to plaster and paint all the buildings at the school.  The chalk boards needs redoing as they are damaged and also very slippery hence it is difficult to write on them.

Objective 2: To construct two new classrooms to be used as the Library and Computer rooms and to also construct three toilet blocks .

The construction will add a toilet block for the boys and second for the girls and the third for the staff. The project will construct two new classroom sized building which will be used as a library and computer room. The present toilet blocks collapsed during the heavy rains the fell in 2006 and they now very unsafe to use. A new site has to be chosen to build the new toilets because the old site is contaminated with human waste.

Objective 3: To established a water supply scheme to supply adequate water to the school, the teacher’s accommodation and the school nutrition garden.

The school is currently using a borehole with a yield of 4 cubic metres per hour which is fitted with a hand pump. The hand pump is very heavy for the young children to pump water out of the borehole and it takes a long time for them to pump enough water to water the school garden. It is proposed that the borehole be fitted with a borehole pump and be powered by a 6metre windmill. The windmill will be connected to pumping mains to transport water to a 13.5 cubic metre storage tank placed on a 12 metre high steel stand. The storage tank connected to reticulation mains that will transport water to the school grounds, teacher’s accommodation and the nutrition garden. The water supply scheme will assist the school to improve the yield of the nutrition garden which in turn will improve the income.

Objective 4: To reduce malnutrition among the children and create financial sustainability by establishing and operating a nutrition garden.

The existing garden is half an acre and is not properly fenced resulting in the crop being destroyed by the local livestock which has affected the school recovery form the garden. It is proposed that the garden be increased to one hectare and be fenced to prevent loss of crop from livestock. New and appropriate garden tools, inputs in the form of seeds and fertiliser be available to improve the productivity of the garden. The children will grow their own food as part of their agriculture lessons and will sell the surplus as a strategy to create financial sustainability for their school.

Objective 5: To reduce adult literacy and enhance life skills to the local youth.

The local community suffers from illiteracy caused by the fact that most children did not pass grade7 and did not continue with their education after that  and have since got married at a young age. Most girl children dropped out of school before completing grade 7, and are now adults. The project proposes that an adult literacy programme be implemented using the local teachers to reduce illiteracy in the community. It is also proposed that youth be targeted with a skills training programme which will them skills in computers, carpentry, building, blacksmith, horticulture, and entrepreneurship.

Objective 6: To promote the children’s participation in sports activities at school and in the community.

It is proposed that the project source sports equipment and make it available to the school and the local community. The project will provide 10 balls, 2 goalpost nets and 2 ball pumps for football, 10 balls, 2 goalpost nets, 2 ball pumps for netball, 2 javelins, 2 discuss disc, 2 shot-puts for athletics. This equipment will be shared between the school and the local community as a way of improving sports in Shamba.

Objective 7: To improve the quality of education through increasing the availability of teaching material

The project will make available teaching aids, text books stationery for the students. It is observed that the current situation is not conducive to good learning. The students come to school without learning tools and they have to share texts books and some of the texts books are old and have some pages missing. Teachers find it difficult to give a test because there is no stationery to duplicate the test and make it available to the students. The project will also source library books to inculcate the culture of reading which currently very low.

Project justification

Shamba is in a very remote rural area where there is no transport and the roads have not been repaired for a long time making it very difficult to access the area. This is a very ambitious project which when complete it is agreed will improve the quality of education at Shamba and will help to improve the grade 7 results which are currently below average. The project hopes to involve the community in the development of the school in particular and the local community in general. Shamba community have experienced a series of natural disasters in the past 10 years. In early 2000 there was a lot of rain that fell over a long time resulting in the destruction of buildings especially toilets and classrooms. Since then the overall economic situation in the country deteriorated and government has since reduced its contribution to the school budget. Many qualified teachers left the country to work in the neighbouring countries at some point leaving Shamba School without teachers.  A number of qualified teachers passed away because of HIV/AIDS related diseases, malaria and cholera.  The situation is slowly improving but still the remaining qualified teachers in the country prefer to work in urban areas where the quality of school buildings and teaching aids are much better. It is planned at completion the project will help attract qualified teachers to the school, improve the quality of education, reduce absenteeism and also reduce the number of girls dropping out of school. The project hopes to encourage the parents to be interested in the education of their children which it is hoped will change their attitudes towards the education of their children and especially the education of the girl child.

The project aims at creating sustainability at the school. It is planned that the development and use of the school garden will assist the school to impart agriculture skills to the students and at the same time the garden will produce enough vegetables for the school to use for school dinners as well as sale the surplus to the local community to raise money to maintain the school.

This project will assist the community to be bankable and will attract other donor organisation to the area. This will assist to raise funds for improving the general infrastructure in the area because donors generally want to work with communities that are well mobilised and those willing to participate in the development of their communities. The funds requested are mostly for inputs, the general labour will be from the community as their contribution to the project.  The project budget is an estimate as it is very difficult to get actual figures because of unstable pricing system in the marketplace.
